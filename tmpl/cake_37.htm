The 1893 Chicago World’s Fair gave the world
the Ferris wheel, Juicy Fruit gum and Shredded
Wheat. Thanks to a very pushy hotel guest, it also
gave us the Brownie.

黑巧克力、黑橄榄与各类干果的融合，介
于饼干和蛋糕之间，像蛋糕般绵软的内心
和巧克力曲奇一样的松脆外衣。

布朗尼的由来有个很有趣的故事，据说有个胖胖的黑
人嬷嬷系着围裙，在厨房烘焙着松软可口的巧克力
蛋糕，可是居然忘了将奶油先打发，这个意外做出
的失败作品本应该要丢掉，但老妈妈一尝，居然十分
美味，布郎尼这个“可爱的错误”就这么成为美国家庭
中最具代表性的蛋糕了。

As if things weren't hectic enough. Guests were arriving from all over the world to marvel
at the wonders of the White City. When into Chicago's Palmer House Hotel walked Bertha
Palmer. Ms. Palmer didn't want any of the desserts on the menu. She wanted something
special for the ladies attending the fair. A piece of cake? 'Smaller. Though still cake-like.
Something that would tuck into the corner of a boxed lunch.' Square? 'Exactly.' What the chefs
created was a cross between a cake and a cookie. A fudgey dense slice of heaven that quickly
caught on among the ladies, and everyone else who sampled it. The Palmer's House brownie
originally came with an apricot glaze and walnuts. We've stripped it down to its lean chocolatey
essentials. Perfect with a glass of ice cold milk.
